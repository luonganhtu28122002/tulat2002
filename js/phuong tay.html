I: WHICH SUBJECTS IN WESTERN CRITICAL CHARACTERISTICS?

1. CALLIGRAPHY.

Calligraphy is an art form whose artwork is texts, sentences, or just a word, a letter that are presented and created in a beautiful and artistic way. In my opinion, Western Calligraphy includes 3 main art disciplines: Calligraphy, Hand - lettering and Typography.

Calligraphy is mainly used in writing and designing text. From the pages of the Bible meticulously handwritten by artisans over the years to the letter written when suddenly thinking of a muse. Calligraphy appears in everything from the simple to the great, Calligraphy is for everyone, even if you are not artistically gifted, whether you write with your left or right hand, even write with your feet, in Any age, you can come to Calligraphy. That's what I love the most, the best thing about Calligraphy.

The focus of Calligraphy is mainly on the design and expression of typefaces, either with brush or nib writing instruments. Calligraphy is a collection of skills and techniques of locating and writing letters, so that letters can express all their integrity, harmony, origin, rhythm and creativity.

2: HAND - LETTERING.

Calligraphy – Modern calligraphy develops from the function of written text: wedding cards, greeting cards, event texts… as well as artistic design: font and typography design, handwritten logo design, Graphic design, calligraphy…

Hand lettering is the art of creating shapes by hand. In the simplest and easiest to understand terms, Calligraphy writes letters, and Hand Lettering draws letters. As an art that evolved from Calligraphy, Hand Lettering is used in the design of headlines, the first letter of a piece of text to make Calligraphy more flowery or stand alone as a work of art. Some Hand lettering works of artist Tri Shiba (https://www.facebook.com/shibadesigner?fref=ts)

3: TYPOGRAPHY.

Typography is the art of arranging and combining letters, Typography is developed based on both Calligraphy and Hand lettering. Different from the above two subjects, Typography is an art that uses both Pen and computer and technology when performing. Composition is the most important thing that makes up a Typograpy work. If Calligraphy designs its text in a horizontal layout, Typography only designs one sentence, one word but in many different layout directions such as wavy, converging, concentric. Typography is used most often in the design of titles, posters, logos and in print. If you are a young person who surfs facebook a lot, you will probably sometimes encounter a black and white photo "so deep" and on it is printed a sentence full of mood, most of which are typography works done by machine. count.

Some Typography works that I ask for permission to get on Vietnamese Typography Fanpage(https://www.facebook.com/groups/typoviet/?fref=ts)

Western Calligraphy is easily recognized by its use of the Latin script - the first set of letters that appeared in Rome around 600 BC.

Calligraphy in Western countries (Western Calligraphy): This art is easily recognizable through the use of the Latin character system - the alphabet first appeared in Rome around 600 BC. Each letter in Western calligraphy has the correct shape, stroke order and the spacing of the letters is very even. You can find features of Western Calligraphy in medieval Western European bibles. The most remarkable feature in this bible is that the first letter of a book or chapter has its own illustration, accompanied by patterns, drawings depicting animals with many colors.

Caligraphy in East Asian countries: In East Asian countries, calligraphy can be considered as the origin of Calligraphy. Calligraphy is most impressive in 4 countries: China, Japan, Korea and Vietnam. We can easily see images of Caligraphy through couplets, murals, especially through images of the masters for the word spring day.

Illustrated calligraphy in medieval Western European bibles has very noticeable accents, for example the first letter of a book or a chapter is given a separate illustrated page. This special page in addition to the letters is also decorated with many colorful patterns and pictures depicting animals.

II: PEN IN CALLIGRAPHY- BASIC GROUPING BY LEVEL.

1: FLAT PEN (STUB PEN or CALLIGRAPHY PEN FLAT STYLE). It was really hard to come up with a name for this group, as I couldn't come up with an exact name after hours of digging through the English literature. Therefore, the names I have given are for reference only, so that everyone can understand, please understand for this inconvenience. Flat pen refers to pens that are flat and horizontal to write with bold strokes depending on the angle of the pen. The number of Flat Pen pens is very diverse, Dip pens, fountain pens, Automatic pen (a type I have just seen but have not tried to write), hard tip pens. A number of Flat pen nibs from Speedball company.

Introducing Kuretake's Flat Pen with stiff bristles, about the Flat Pen with stiff bristles, I recommend everyone to use this, around 30k and stay away from Artline and Fastell pens.

2 FLEX PEN: Flex Pen is a group of pens with a flexible pointy nib that can be split apart to create bold strokes according to the pressure of the nib. Because the width of the bold bar is determined by the pressure of the hand, it is considered to be more difficult to write than the Flat pen. Two typical Flex pen typefaces are Copperplate script and Spencerian script. Some Flex pen nibs of SpeedballCopperplate script are a familiar typeface for Vietnamese people, bold bar typeface is considered a variant Font of Copperplate. The principle of writing Copperplate is that the up stroke is the bar stroke, the down stroke is the bold stroke. The more even the text, the clearer the bold bars, the better the text. Copperplate script template. Spencercian Scrpit is a typeface for fast writing by only bolding important points in a word, thus speeding up writing and reducing hand fatigue. Spencarcian script font template.

3: BRUSH PEN Brush Pen is a brush used in Calligraphy, it includes 2 types: soft nib and hard nib. The soft nib is that the entire nib is very soft, and the hard nib is only the tip of the soft nib, the root of the nib is treated with glue, so it is very hard, so when pressed with full force, the stroke width does not change. The most important feature of the Brush Pen is that the nib is very soft, so it is difficult to control the nib. A small change, a change of breathing is enough to change the brush stroke compared to normal, so the Brush Pen is considered a difficult pen to write and only those at the Master level can write hundreds of words evenly. as one. Because of this property, hard nibs are much easier to write than soft nibs. Unlike Flat pen when writing must adhere to strict guidelines, Brush Pen is very customizable and is therefore popularly used in Typography. As I mentioned above, the most important thing in Typography is composition, and the Brush Pen offers the ability to change the angle of inclination customarily according to the composition of the work. Introducing the Brush Pen that is very popular among young Saigonese today, the Tombow Dual Brush Pen.

4: Rolling Pen. Rulling Pen, the pinnacle pen for Masters. Due to the nature of the extremely wide nib, much ink down, difficult to control, Rulling Pen is the most difficult pen to write and especially there are never two Identical letters. Even Master Calligraphers cannot write two words exactly the same. A Rulling Pen can have a lock to control the opening of the nib, because the ink drops quickly, so when writing, the writer has to emphasize and swipe very fast, so scratching the paper is also a lot. Therefore, the Rulling Pen writing paper is about 300gsm thick. Some Ruling Pen nibs.

Calligraphy - Western calligraphy is more about the rules of the shape of letters. The spacing between the letters is rhythmic and evenly spaced, with certain "geometric" compositions forming through the straight lines on the page. Each letter has a very strict stroke order.
