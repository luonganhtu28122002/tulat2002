A guide to learning Vietnamese calligraphy for beginners.

learning write : lesson 1: group a,d,v

Stroke 1. The trend is slightly left leaning, the distance is more than 1 cell, can be created with Phi Bach stroke or a simple pen. Learn more about the benefits of practicing calligraphy.

Stroke 2: Vertical trend, 2 cells in length, made up of pen or non-white strokes. Note: the distance between the 2 strokes is narrow. The strokes are strong, decisive, and the pen is round.

Stroke 3: curvy line, starting with a pen stroke, gradually lifting it as you go, creating a beautiful scratch

2. the letter d

Stroke 1: space of 1 cell, written in Phi Bach stroke.

Stroke 2: starting from the end of stroke 1, going with the tip of the pen, thin strokes, both parallel and close to stroke 1. Raise to the height of 2 cells, then go down to the large stroke, retract the pen. Learn and refer to more about how to combine calligraphy with short horizontal, long and vertical forms.

Note: The width of stroke 1 and stroke 2 is quite small, and must be parallel to be beautiful. The finger-twist recording is easier to do.

3. the letter v

Go by 2 strokes.

Stroke 1: 1 cell high, slightly left leaning tendency, big stroke

Stroke 2, slightly vertical trend, thin stroke, height 5/4 or 2 cells

Attention: the distance N1 and N2 is small. Avoid the error of leaning too much

how to learn to write calligraphy ?

Calligraphy isn't difficult, so you can take some online classes. Or you can join beautiful calligraphy classes . Although learning to write is not difficult, you still have to pay attention to how to put the basic strokes. How to stop the pen and collect the pen. Some use some effective calligraphy tools such as: brush, ink research, ...
