The end of the Literati paintings

The North Branch of the National Palace Museum kicked off the "Last stroke of Literati Painting - Pu Xinshe's Calligraphy and Painting Special Exhibition" from today, which will display calligraphy and painting works. painting by the almighty calligrapher and painter Pu Ru. rich in content and displayed at the first exhibition area 202, 204, 206, 210, 212 and other galleries, the exhibition will last until the end of December 21 (Tuesday). North courtyard of the Palace Museum.

Pu Ru (1896 ~ 1963), courtesy name Xinshe, was born into the Manchu royal family of the Qing Dynasty, and even participated in the selection of emperors after the death of Emperor Guangxu. The rich collection of ancient calligraphy and painting in the home of Crown Prince Gong of Pu Xinshe, who considers himself integrated and has become a master of all calligraphy styles, such as line, cursive, seals, and letters, as well as paintings of landscapes, stalls, figures of people, flowers, birds, and animals. The almighty calligrapher.

His profound knowledge of poetry, calligraphy and painting, coupled with a profoundly erudite style, made him the last painter in art history, although he did not become the last emperor.
